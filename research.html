Research

From protein to patterned device

Our work runs from chemistry — making natural biopolymers respond to light — through microfabrication, mechanical design, and finally functional devices tested against cells, serum, and skin.

Photoreactive biomaterials

Making natural polymers behave like photoresists without giving up what makes them biomaterials.

Photofibroin and photosericin

Methacrylated silk fibroin (photofibroin, 2013) and methacrylated silk sericin (photosericin, 2015) are protein resists that crosslink under light. They show enhanced stability in aqueous environments, preserve microscale architecture, and are mechanically tunable. The fabrication route is entirely green and protein-based. Photosericin has been licensed to Advanced BioMatrix.

Advanced Materials 2013, 2014 · US Patent 9,771,400

Elastomeric photofibroin

Doping photofibroin with under 5% UV-curable siloxane yields extraordinary strength and versatility while retaining cytocompatibility and biodegradation over roughly a month. These substrates support silver circuit patterning and are compatible with traditional microfabrication.

ACS Applied Bio Materials 2023

Gelatin composites

Gelatin resembles native extracellular matrix and is inexpensive and versatile, but it is unstable at biological temperature and mechanically weak. We form large-area, optically transparent sheets via enzymatic crosslinking with microbial transglutaminase (MxGel), add PEDOT:PSS for electroconductive films (CMxGel), and use polydopamine coatings to improve strength and elasticity. Combined with photofibroin, these give versatile substrates for functional electronics and cellular engineering.

ACS Applied Bio Materials 2022 · ACS Biomaterials Science & Engineering 2023

Patterning and fabrication

Spatial control across five orders of magnitude, using intrinsic material properties and adding new function.

Nanoscale patterning

Electron beam lithography forms protein nanowires and nanostructures, made conductive with PEDOT:PSS.

Macromolecular Materials and Engineering 2017 · Nanotechnology 2018

2D formats

Flexible micropatterned sheets and shape-defined particles produced through photolithographic patterning of protein resists.

ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces 2015 · European Polymer Journal 2016

3D printing

Photofibroin and its composites are 3D printed from an aqueous solvent via DLP, producing centimeter-scale structures with internal voids that compress and recover. Conducting hydrogels combining photofibroin with PEDOT:PSS are electrochemically characterized and cytocompatible with fibroblasts and myoblasts. Molded microneedles show enhanced wet adhesion with calcium chloride.

Advanced Engineering Materials 2025

Kirigami mechanics

Cuts, not chemistry, as the design variable for compliance at intrinsically dynamic biointerfaces.

Kirigami-inspired films

Introducing engineered cuts into conductive fibroin sheets improves conformability, flexibility, and diffusion. Films load small molecules such as FITC-dextran, and C2C12 cells adhere and form cytoskeletal bridges across the cuts over a week of culture.

ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces 2020

Computational design of cuts

Finite element modeling visualizes stress concentration near cut tips under increasing load for slit and branched (Y-shaped) geometries, letting us design cuts or material openings to target specific levels of stretch and modes of bending before anything is fabricated. In collaboration with N. Pugno, University of Trento.

Cell stacking in nanosheets

Kirigami sheets stack into millimeter-thick macroscale constructs where the cut design aids nutrient and oxygen transport to inner layers. C2C12 myoblasts differentiate over three weeks into elongated, multinucleated myotubes expressing sarcomeric alpha actin, and electrical stimulation provides actuation.

ACS Biomaterials Science & Engineering 2023 · work in progress, 2025

Flexible bioelectronics

Water-based, room-temperature, bench-top processes yielding fully organic circuits and transient devices.

Conductive silk inks

Photosericin combined with PEDOT:PSS gives flexible, fully organic circuits printed at room temperature from water. A dual-function ink incorporating iron oxide nanoparticles (PSION) writes circuits that also act as magnetic connect/disconnect nodes.

Advanced Materials 2016 · Organic Electronics 2024 · US Patent allowance, Nov 2025

Biosensors

Selective and non-selective electrochemical biosensors built from conductive ink plus glucose oxidase, with roughly 30-second response times: ascorbic acid linear to 600 µM at 256.5 nA/µM cm²; dopamine linear to 200 µM at 45.9 nA/µM cm²; glucose linear to 8 mM at 7.57 µA/mM cm² with under 0.5% enzyme leaching after three days. Flexible impedimetric sensors detect VEGF in human serum and in urine with and without albumin.

Biosensors & Bioelectronics 2016 · Sensors and Actuators B 2017 · ACS Sensors 2019

Temperature sensing and energy storage

Skin-mounted flexible temperature sensors weighing about 3.7 mg/cm², alongside biopolymer micro-supercapacitors roughly 27 times lighter than office paper. Both degrade in an enzymatic (protease) environment over about 21 days while remaining stable in plain aqueous conditions — degradation on demand rather than by accident.

ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces 2018 · ACS Applied Electronic Materials 2021

Cellular engineering

Cell micropatterning

Photofibroin micropatterns direct selective osteoblast adhesion to 10 µm lines and support L929 fibroblast culture. The materials and patterns degrade enzymatically in a controllable way.

Advanced Materials Interfaces 2019

Electrical stimulation

Covalently attached organic bioink patterns align cells and deliver stimulation. Over two weeks, stimulation increases myotube width and length — indicators of cell fusion and development.

ACS Biomaterials Science & Engineering 2023

Biosecurity and authentication

Protein self-assembly as a stochastic image generator

Silk species dictate architectural variation in diffusion-limited aggregation: Bombyx mori gives dendritic architectures, Antheraea mylitta orthogonal ones, and Antheraea assamensis spherulites. Binarized nanoscale images of this self-assembly generate cryptographic keys for enrolment, storage, and later validation — randomness in nature turned into a physically unclonable function.

Printed security labels

Maskless, rapid digital printing places a high-contrast photosericin bioink pattern on a photofibroin sheet, with fiduciary markers accounting for distortion, lighting, and camera resolution. The flexible sheets adhere to non-planar geometry and degrade proteolytically in ten days. Applications include tamper-evident labels, secure QR codes, anti-counterfeiting for pharmaceuticals, and product tracing under the Safeguarding Therapeutics Act of 2020.
